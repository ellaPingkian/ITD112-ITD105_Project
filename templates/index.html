Philippine Dengue Cases and Deaths 2016-2021

The dataset contains confirmed dengue cases and deaths from the Department of Health-Epidemiology Bureau in the Philippines in the years 2016-2021.

Notes: Reported dengue cases includes suspect, probable and confirmed. Deaths were included in the total number of cases.

TABLE 1: This shows the cases and deaths recorded in the Philippines in 2016-2021 per region, and also shows the mortality rate over the cases recorded. The highest recorded cases are tallied in Region IV-A Calabarzon with a total of 163,177, yet its total deaths recorded is only 653 which makes it 0.4% for the mortality rate of dengue cases in the said region. On the other hand, the highest recorded deaths are tallied in National Capital Region (NCR) with a total of 4009 deaths over 116,147 cases which makes it the second highest mortality rate of all the region recorded.

TABLE 2: This shows the total number of deaths and cases recorded per year in the span of 6 years (2016-2021), and also the mortality rate tallied in each year. Highest recorded cases were in 2019 with a total of 441,902 cases in that year alone with only 1,733 deaths recorded. Although the cases recorded increased by 287,747 in the span of 3 years, that is between 2017-2019, the deaths recorded decreases as the from 8,127 to 8 from 2016 to first month of 2021. This only means that the Department of Health-Epidemiology Bureau in the Philippines handles the dengue cases better in the recent years.
